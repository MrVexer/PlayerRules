Definitions

IC - In Character: Anything your character knows, says, or experiences.
OOC - Out of Character: Anything outside of roleplay including Discord, voice chat, streams, and real-life information.
Metagaming: Using OOC information that your character would not reasonably know.
Powergaming: Forcing actions onto another player or performing unrealistic actions without allowing interaction.
Random Deathmatch - RDM: Attacking or killing another player without valid in-character reason.
Find Out In Character - FOIC: Gaining knowledge through roleplay, not through chat or Discord.
Combat Logging: Disconnecting to avoid roleplay or combat.
FailRP: Acting in a way that ignores realistic roleplay expectations.
NLR - New Life Rule: NLR Takes affect after your death.
NVL - No Value of Life: Acting with little or no regard for your character's own survival
NVL PK - NVL Player Kill: A permanent character death issued by staff when a player displays extreme No Value of Life

1.0 Community Rules

Rule 1.1 - Respect Everyone

Treat every member with respect. No personal attacks, harassment, racism, sexism, homophobia, or hate speech. Keep disagreements civil.

Rule 1.2 - No Toxic Behaviour

Do not troll, intentionally provoke players, constantly interrupt RP, grief events, or ruin other players' experiences.

Rule 1.3 - Staff Respect

Staff decisions should be respected. If you disagree, finish the situation and appeal later. Do not argue in-game.

Rule 1.4 - No Rule Lawyering or Loophole Abuse

The rules cannot cover every possible situation. Deliberately exploiting technicalities, mechanics, or omissions in the rules to gain an unfair advantage is prohibited. Staff will always enforce the spirit of the rules over the literal wording.

Rule 1.5 - Staff Discretion

Staff may intervene in any situation they believe is harmful to the server, roleplay, player experience, or community, even if the specific behaviour is not explicitly listed in these rules.

2.0 Character Rules

Rule 2.1 - Character Names

Names must fit Elder Scrolls lore.

Allowed: Bjorn Ironhand, Decimus Corvinus, Ra'zirr

Not Allowed: John Wick, Naruto, Batman

Rule 2.2 - Character Background

Every character should have a believable history, believable goals, and a believable personality.

Rule 2.3 - Value Your Life

Your character values their own life. Do not charge twenty guards alone, fight impossible odds, ignore serious injuries, or suicide to escape RP.

Rule 2.4 - Fear Roleplay

React realistically when threatened. If a bandit points a sword at your neck, surrendering or negotiating is realistic. Laughing and attacking anyway is not.

Rule 2.5 - Character Consistency

Remain consistent. Do not completely change your character's personality simply because it benefits you.

Rule 2.6 - Character Knowledge

Your character only knows what they have personally learned through roleplay. Relationships, criminal activity, political events, and personal information must all be discovered through IC interactions.

Rule 2.7 - Character Separation

Information, money, items, influence, or relationships may not be transferred between your own characters unless approved through legitimate roleplay. Each character is considered an independent individual.

3.0 Roleplay Standards

Rule 3.1 - Quality Roleplay

Players should focus on creating enjoyable stories rather than winning.

Rule 3.2 - No Meme RP

Avoid modern jokes, internet slang, breaking immersion, and unrealistic behaviour.

Rule 3.3 - Realistic Injuries

Injuries matter. Broken bones, arrows, and serious wounds should affect roleplay.

Rule 3.4 - Consequences

Every action has consequences. Stealing, murder, assault, and political decisions should all create lasting roleplay.

Rule 3.5 - Consent RP

ERP, torture, permanent injury, and similar roleplay require the consent of everyone involved.

4.0 Communication Rules

Rule 4.1 - Stay In Character

Remain IC whenever possible. Use OOC chat only when necessary.

Rule 4.2 - Voice Communication

Do not ear rape, spam sounds, play music, or use soundboards unless approved for an event.

Rule 4.3 - OOC Arguments

Never argue OOC inside active roleplay.

5.0 New Life Rule - NLR

If your character dies:

  • Previous events are forgotten from the last 30 minutes.
  • You cannot immediately return to the area where you died unless it is your home or place of work.
  • You cannot seek revenge using information gained during the 30 minutes prior to your death.
  • Exceptions may exist for GM staff-approved events.

6.0 Metagaming & Powergaming

Rule 6.1 - No Metagaming

You may not use Discord information, streams, friends giving locations outside of local VC/local text, or OOC conversations to benefit IC.

Rule 6.2 - No Powergaming

Do not force actions onto another player or use mechanics in a way that removes reasonable roleplay interaction.

Example: using cuffs on a player when you have no known IC reason or crime to justify it.

7.0 Combat Rules

Rule 7.1 - Valid Initiation

Combat requires legitimate roleplay. There must be motive, escalation, and interaction before violence begins.

Rule 7.2 - No Random Killing

Killing requires a clear RP reason and believable escalation.

Rule 7.3 - Hostages

Hostages must be treated realistically. Random executions are prohibited.

Rule 7.4 - Combat Logging

Logging out to avoid combat results in punishment.

8.0 Crime Rules

Rule 8.1 - Crime Must Create RP

Crime should create stories, not easy money.

Rule 8.2 - Theft

Theft must involve roleplay. Do not steal simply because mechanics allow it.

Rule 8.3 - Murder

Murder requires strong IC justification. Repeated random murders are FailRP.

Rule 8.4 - Banditry

Bandits must interact, negotiate, and roleplay before violence.

9.0 Government & Laws

Rule 9.1 - Respect Officials

Guards and government officials deserve realistic respect during interactions. Failure to do so may result in arrest.

Rule 9.2 - Corruption

Corruption must make sense and be staff approved. It cannot exist solely to abuse players.

Rule 9.3 - Warrants & Arrests

Guards should follow server laws and procedures whenever possible.

10.0 Economy Rules

Rule 10.1 - No Duplication

Duplicating items or money is strictly prohibited.

Rule 10.2 - Fair Trading

Scams that rely on game mechanics rather than roleplay are prohibited.

Rule 10.3 - No Exploiting Prices

Do not abuse bugs to generate wealth.

11.0 Safe Zones - Temples

Unless otherwise stated, designated safe zones prohibit random violence, combat initiation, harassment and combat baiting.

Players may not use safe zones to avoid ongoing roleplay, evade law enforcement, escape active combat or intentionally disrupt interactions already in progress.

12.0 Exploits & Bugs

Players may never abuse bugs, duplicate items, clip through walls, abuse animations, or exploit glitches.

Report bugs immediately.

13.0 Reporting Players

If someone breaks rules:

  • Record evidence.
  • Finish the RP if possible.
  • Submit a report.
  • Do not argue publicly.

14.0 Streaming & Recording

Streaming is encouraged. However:

  • Hide confidential staff discussions.
  • Do not stream snipe.
  • Do not encourage harassment.
  • Respect privacy requests where appropriate.

    • Stream sniping, intentionally locating players through broadcasts, or using livestream information for in-character benefit is considered metagaming.

15.0 Corruption

Corruption is permitted when it is believable, lore-friendly, and intended to create meaningful roleplay. Examples of corruption may include accepting bribes or turning a blind eye to minor crimes. The following rules apply:

  • Corruption must have a legitimate in-character reason and should fit your character's personality, background, and position.
  • Corrupt actions must create roleplay opportunities, not remove them. Other players should have a reasonable chance to uncover, investigate, or respond to your actions.
  • Players may not use corruption as an excuse to grief, harass, repeatedly target others, or bypass server rules.
  • Positions of authority (such as Guards, Government Officials, Court Members, or other faction leaders) may engage in limited corruption, but they are expected to perform their duties. Constant abuse of power or treating corruption as the norm is not permitted.
  • Corruption should carry realistic in-character risks and consequences. If your actions are discovered, you are expected to roleplay the resulting investigations, arrests, loss of position, or other appropriate outcomes.
  • Staff may intervene if corruption is deemed excessive, unrealistic, or harmful to the overall roleplay experience.

16.0 ERP (Erotic Roleplay)

Romantic roleplay between consenting players is permitted.

Explicit sexual roleplay, simulated sexual acts, nudity, strip clubs, prostitution, or similar adult-themed content is prohibited.

Any unwanted romantic or sexual roleplay directed at another player will be treated as harassment and may result in a permanent ban.

17.0 Robbery & Scams

Robbery and scams are permitted when they are used to create meaningful and immersive roleplay. They must never be used solely to generate easy profit or ruin another player's experience.

    Robbery

  • You must have a valid in-character reason to rob another player or business.
  • Robberies must provide the victim with a reasonable opportunity to comply, negotiate, resist, or attempt to escape where appropriate.
  • Randomly robbing players without roleplay or justification is not permitted.
  • Excessive or repeated robberies targeting the same player or group may be considered harassment.
  • Players may not force victims to hand over all of their belongings unless the situation realistically justifies it. Maximum of 20% of the victims gold and 2 equipped items can be stolen at a time
  • If a victim fully complies and poses no threat, unnecessary violence or execution is not permitted unless there is a clear in-character reason.

    • Scamming

  • In-character scams, fraud, false contracts, counterfeit goods, and deception are permitted when they are believable and roleplay-focused.
  • Players may lie, manipulate, or deceive others in-character, provided the victim has a fair opportunity to identify the deception through roleplay.
  • Scams that rely on exploiting game mechanics, bugs, UI limitations, or a player's lack of game knowledge are strictly prohibited.
  • Out-of-character deception (misleading players through Discord or OOC messages) is never permitted.

    • Staff Discretion

  • If a robbery or scam is considered unrealistic, excessively one-sided, or intended primarily to grief or generate easy profit rather than roleplay, staff may intervene and issue appropriate punishments.

Provoking Lore Factions/h2>

Players are expected to value their character's life and act in a believable manner when interacting with major lore factions (such as the City Guards, Imperial Legion, Stormcloaks, Thalmor, Vigilants of Stendarr, and similar organisations). Provoking, attacking, or deliberately antagonising these factions without a compelling in-character reason is prohibited.

Examples of valid reasons include:

  • Acting in genuine self-defence.
  • Attempting to protect or rescue another character.
  • Following an established character arc or ongoing roleplay.
  • Acting under the orders of your faction or as part of an approved event.

    • Examples of invalid reasons include:

  • Attacking guards "for fun."
  • Starting fights simply to create PvP.
  • Intentionally provoking large factions with no realistic expectation of survival.
  • Creating conflict solely to obtain a PK or force roleplay on others.

    • Characters who choose to provoke or attack a powerful lore faction without a believable reason may be considered to have shown No Value of Life (NVL). Staff may issue an NVL Player Kill (PK) or other appropriate punishments where warranted.

General Expectations

By playing on the server you agree to:

  • Create immersive stories.
  • Respect fellow players.
  • Accept both victory and defeat.
  • Value roleplay over winning.
  • Help maintain a welcoming community.

Final Note

Staff may enforce punishments if they believe a player's actions are not in the best interests of the server, roleplay, lore, or overall player experience, even if the specific behaviour is not explicitly covered by these rules.

The intent and spirit of the rules always take precedence over attempts to exploit loopholes or technicalities.
